> **Interactive Solar Magnetic Reconnection Simulator**

It combines scientific principles with interactive visualization to make one of the Sun's most fascinating physical processes easier to understand.

## 🌞 About the Sun

The Sun is a G-type main-sequence star composed primarily of hydrogen and helium. It consists of extremely hot ionized plasma, where charged particles interact with powerful magnetic fields. These magnetic fields interact with the solar plasma, driving dynamic processes throughout the Sun's atmosphere.

---

## 🧲 Magnetic Reconnection

Magnetic reconnection is a physical process in which oppositely directed magnetic field lines break and reconnect, converting stored magnetic energy into kinetic and thermal energy within the surrounding plasma.

Helios provides a simplified interactive visualization of this phenomenon, allowing users to explore the relationship between magnetic fields, plasma, and energy.

---

## ⚡ Plasma

Plasma is the fourth state of matter, consisting of freely moving ions and electrons. Unlike solids, liquids, or gases, plasma is highly responsive to electric and magnetic fields. Since the Sun is primarily composed of plasma, its behavior is largely governed by magnetic interactions.

---

## 📐 Physical Principles

Helios is based on fundamental concepts from electromagnetism and plasma physics, including:

### Lorentz Force

**F = q(E + v × B)**

Determines the force acting on charged particles moving through electric and magnetic fields.

---

### Magnetic Pressure

**P = B² / (2μ₀)**

Represents the pressure exerted by a magnetic field on the surrounding plasma.

---

### Magnetic Energy Density

**u = B² / (2μ₀)**

Describes the magnetic energy stored per unit volume.

---

### Magnetic Flux

**Φ = ∫ B · dA**

Measures the total magnetic field passing through a surface.

## ⚠️ Scientific Note

Helios is an educational visualization based on the principles of solar magnetic reconnection. It demonstrates the interaction between magnetic fields, plasma, and energy using simplified physical models and interactive visualization. It is **not** intended to be a full magnetohydrodynamic (MHD) numerical simulation.
